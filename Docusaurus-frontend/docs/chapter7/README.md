## Chapter 7: Capstone Project & Assessments

This culminating chapter provides the framework for your Capstone Project, offering an opportunity to synthesize the knowledge and skills acquired throughout the textbook. Here, you'll transition from guided learning to independent application, tackling real-world challenges in Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics. We also include comprehensive assessments to solidify your understanding and evaluate your progress.

### Learning Strata and Objectives:

| Strata      | Learning Objectives                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| :---------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Normal**    | - Successfully complete a guided capstone project integrating ROS packages, Gazebo simulations, and basic AI pipelines. <br/> - Apply learned concepts to solve a defined robotic problem. <br/> - Present project outcomes clearly. |

| **Advanced**  | - Develop a novel capstone project addressing an open problem in Physical AI or Humanoid Robotics. <br/> - Implement cutting-edge techniques (e.g., edge AI for real-time VLA) in your project. <br/> - Conduct rigorous evaluation and present research-grade findings. |

### 7.1 The Capstone Project: Synthesizing Your Knowledge

The Capstone Project is designed to be the ultimate test of your understanding and practical skills. It provides a structured yet flexible environment for you to apply everything you've learned.

**Normal**: Your capstone project will involve a guided integration of previously learned concepts. This could include:

*   **Voice-to-Action Path Planning**: Developing a system where a verbal command translates into a robot planning and executing a path in a simulated environment.
*   **Object Manipulation**: Programming a robot arm to grasp and move objects based on visual input within a Gazebo or Isaac Sim environment.

You'll utilize ROS packages for communication, Gazebo or Isaac Sim for simulation, and basic AI pipelines (e.g., simple image processing) for perception. Implementation guides will provide step-by-step instructions and best practices.

**Pro**: For those ready to push further, your project will involve more complex AI-powered perception (e.g., advanced Isaac pipelines from Chapter 4) and sophisticated control strategies. This will require deeper integration of modules, performance optimization, and robust error handling.

**Advanced**: This stratum encourages you to tackle novel problems. Consider topics like:

*   **Edge AI for Real-Time VLA**: Implementing a Visual-Language-Action system (from Chapter 6) that runs efficiently on an edge device (like NVIDIA Jetson), enabling real-time conversational and physical interaction.
*   **Humanoid Task Automation**: Designing an AI system for a humanoid robot to autonomously perform a series of tasks, adapting to dynamic environments.

**Researcher**: The capstone project for researchers should aim to contribute to the field. This could involve:

*   **ML Ablation Studies**: Systematically removing or modifying components of an AI model to understand their individual contributions to overall performance.
*   **Extension to existing research**: Building upon published work in Physical AI or Humanoid Robotics.
*   **Exploration of ethical dilemmas**: Designing a system to highlight or mitigate ethical biases in embodied AI.

### 7.2 Comprehensive Assessments

Throughout the textbook, quizzes and practical exercises have helped reinforce your learning. This chapter provides a consolidated view of potential assessments to validate your mastery.

*   **Isaac Pipelines with Rubrics**: For projects leveraging NVIDIA Isaac, detailed rubrics will be provided to evaluate the efficiency, robustness, and creativity of your AI implementations.
*   **ROS Package Deployment**: Assessment of your ability to create, build, and deploy functional ROS packages that interact with simulated or physical hardware.
*   **Gazebo Simulation Scenarios**: Evaluation based on your ability to set up, run, and analyze robotic behavior in complex Gazebo simulation scenarios.

### Contextual Enrichment & Hardware Tiers

*   **Hardware Tiers**: The scope and complexity of your capstone project may depend on your hardware access. For advanced projects involving bipedal locomotion or sophisticated manipulation, platforms like the **Unitree G1 with its SDK security features** offer robust capabilities. Consider utilizing cloud-based simulated proxies as a fallback for projects requiring high-end hardware.
*   **Personalization Hooks**: The project ideas and assessment criteria are designed with personalization in mind. If your background is stronger in ML, you might focus on the AI model development. If it's in robotics, the integration and hardware aspects might be your primary focus.
